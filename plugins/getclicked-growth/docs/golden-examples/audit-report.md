## Critical issues (fix immediately)

### 1. Missing meta descriptions on 60% of pages

**Impact:** Google is auto-generating descriptions from page content for 9 of 15 indexed pages. Auto-generated snippets average 1.8% CTR vs. 3.2% for pages with custom descriptions (industry benchmark). At Invincible's current impression volume (~2,400/mo), that's an estimated 34 lost clicks per month -- and the gap widens as organic traffic grows.

**Evidence:** Pages missing meta descriptions include `/features`, `/pricing`, `/about`, `/blog/504-plan-guide`, and all 4 blog posts. The homepage and `/demo` have descriptions but they're truncated at 180 characters (should be 150-160).

**Fix:**
1. Write unique meta descriptions for all 15 indexed pages. Lead with the benefit, include a verb, stay under 155 characters.
2. For the 504 blog post (your highest-traffic page), use: "What is a 504 plan? Learn what it covers, who qualifies, and how to make sure your child's plan is actually followed every day."
3. Truncate homepage and `/demo` descriptions to 155 characters.

**Effort:** Low -- 2-3 hours of copywriting. No dev work required if using Webflow CMS.

### 2. Homepage mobile load time: 4.2 seconds

**Impact:** Google's Core Web Vitals threshold is 2.5 seconds for Largest Contentful Paint (LCP). At 4.2s, the homepage fails CWV on mobile, which affects both rankings and bounce rate. Mobile users (48% of Invincible's traffic) are 2.5x more likely to bounce at >3s load time.

**Evidence:** Lighthouse mobile score: 58. LCP: 4.2s. Primary culprits: hero image (2.1MB uncompressed PNG), two render-blocking JavaScript files from Webflow interactions, and no lazy loading on below-fold images.

**Fix:**
1. Compress hero image to WebP format (target <200KB). Webflow supports WebP natively.
2. Add `loading="lazy"` to all below-fold images (6 images on homepage).
3. Defer non-critical Webflow interactions JavaScript. Move interaction scripts to page bottom or add `defer` attribute.
4. Target: LCP under 2.5s, Lighthouse mobile score >75.

**Effort:** Medium -- 3-4 hours. Requires Webflow CMS access and basic image optimization.

## Warnings (fix this month)

<details>
<summary><strong>No structured data (schema markup) on any page</strong> -- missing FAQ, Product, and Organization schema</summary>

**Impact:** Structured data enables rich snippets in search results -- FAQ dropdowns, star ratings, product details. The 504 blog post has 5 FAQ-style questions that could render as FAQ rich snippets, potentially doubling the SERP real estate for that result.

**Evidence:** Tested all 15 pages via Google's Rich Results Test. Zero structured data detected. Competitors Go504 and Magnus Health both have Organization schema; Go504 also has FAQ schema on their glossary pages.

**Fix:**
1. Add FAQPage schema to the 504 blog post (5 questions already in the content).
2. Add Organization schema to the homepage (name, URL, logo, social profiles, founding date).
3. Add Product schema to `/pricing` (3 tiers with pricing).

**Effort:** Medium -- 2-3 hours. Can be added via Webflow custom code or a JSON-LD script in the page head.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Blog post internal linking is weak</strong> -- 504 guide has zero internal links to product pages</summary>

**Impact:** The "What is a 504 plan?" blog post is the highest-traffic page on the site (estimated 400+ monthly visits). It links to zero product pages. Every visitor who reads the guide and wants to learn about Invincible has to navigate manually. That's a conversion leak.

**Evidence:** The blog post has 3 external links (to ed.gov, wrightslaw.org, and the OCR complaint page) but zero internal links to `/features`, `/pricing`, or `/demo`. The closing paragraph mentions "tools that help coordinate daily care" but doesn't link to Invincible's product.

**Fix:**
1. Add 2-3 contextual internal links within the blog post body. Example: "Tools like Invincible help care teams [track daily 504 plan execution](/features) by giving nurses, teachers, and parents a shared timeline."
2. Add a CTA block at the end of the post: "See how Invincible helps schools execute 504 plans daily" with a link to `/demo`.
3. For future blog posts, establish a minimum of 2 internal links per article.

**Effort:** Low -- 30 minutes.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Missing alt text on 40% of images</strong> -- 12 of 30 images site-wide lack descriptive alt attributes</summary>

**Impact:** Alt text serves two purposes: accessibility (screen readers) and SEO (image search). Missing alt text means 12 images contribute nothing to search rankings and the site fails WCAG 2.1 Level A accessibility standards. For an education product, accessibility compliance matters to buyers.

**Evidence:** Homepage hero image, 3 feature screenshots, and 8 blog post images have either empty alt attributes or generic "image" text.

**Fix:** Write descriptive alt text for all 12 images. Be specific: "School nurse logging a 504 care note on a tablet in Invincible's student logbook" is better than "screenshot" or "nurse image."

**Effort:** Low -- 1 hour.

</details>
