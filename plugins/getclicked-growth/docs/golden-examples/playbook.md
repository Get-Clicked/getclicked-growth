## 1. The Company

Invincible is a FERPA-compliant communication platform for school health teams and families of students with chronic and behavioral health conditions. Founded in 2019 by Bob Weishar (diagnosed with type 1 diabetes at 18), built from a year of interviews with 100+ families and school staff.

Three features: **Student Logbook** (shared care timeline), **Alerts and Follow-Ups** (tied to 504/behavior plans), and **Secure Parent Messaging** (no app download required). Priced at $0 / $1,500 / $7,500 per year.

The product fills a gap no competitor touches: compliance tools write the plan document, health record tools store forms, communication tools broadcast announcements. Nobody manages the plan in *action* -- daily coordination between nurse, teacher, counselor, and parent.

## 2. The Market

**Size:** ~130,000 K-12 schools in the US. 3-4 million students with 504 plans (growing faster than IEPs). $35B annual EdTech K-12 spend. Buying cycle: January-May decisions for fall implementation.

**Three adjacent categories, none doing what Invincible does:**

| Category | Key Players | What They Do | Their Gap |
|----------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| 504/IEP Compliance | Frontline, Embrace, Go504, SpedTrack | Write the plan document | Don't touch daily execution |
| School Health Records | Magnus Health, SNAP | Digitize immunizations, physicals | Static records, no real-time coordination |
| School Communication | Remind, ClassDojo, ParentSquare | Broadcast announcements | Not health-specific, not FERPA-compliant |

**Tailwinds:** OCR complaints rising (more compliance scrutiny), MTSS adoption expanding (documentation requirements), post-COVID mental health surge (more 504 plans than ever).

## 3. The Customer

**Three personas, one buying motion:**

**District SPED Director** (primary buyer) -- F, 40-55, M.Ed/Ed.D, oversees compliance across 5-50+ buildings. Buys after an OCR complaint or parent escalation. Key pain: "Can't answer whether 504 plans are being followed without calling the building." Contract: $7,500/yr.

**Private School Admin** (secondary buyer) -- Head of school or student services director. Tuition-paying parents expect premium communication. Key pain: "Our nurse texts parents from her personal phone. When she's out, nobody knows what to do." Contract: $1,500/yr.

**Parent Champion** (influencer, not buyer) -- Parent of a child with ADHD, diabetes, anxiety. Searches "504 plan for ADHD" (1,900/mo). Discovers Invincible through content, then asks their school "why don't we have this?" This is the growth loop catalyst.

## 5. The Channels

Bullseye framework identified three inner-ring channels:

**1. 504 Content Engine (SEO) -- Score: 31/35.** The growth loop: parent searches "504 plan for ADHD" -> finds Invincible's guide -> shares with school -> school evaluates -> school signs up. 60,500 monthly searches for "504 plan" with competition index 3-7. Nobody owns this content authoritatively. Estimated CAC: $50-150 (amortized content production). This is the durable, compounding engine.

**2. Google Ads (SEM) -- Score: 25/35.** Already running at $3K/mo across 3 ad groups. Captures high-intent "SPED software" and "IEP software" buyers plus competitor intercept. Ceiling is low (~$5K/mo before diminishing returns), but it's the immediate pipeline while content builds.

**3. Conference Speaking -- Score: 24/35.** Bob's founding story is a conference talk, not a sales pitch. District SPED directors discover tools through peer recommendations. Targets: CEC, NASET, NAIS, and state-level SPED conferences. 5-20 qualified conversations per event.

**Middle ring (next quarter):** Partnerships with compliance vendors (28/35 -- needs traction proof first), community building in parent Facebook groups, email marketing once content drives 1,000+ monthly visitors.

## 9. The Experiments

**EXP-001: 504 Plan Execution -- Launch Campaign** (Status: Design)

The foundational test. Validates whether the "execution gap" positioning generates demo bookings via Google Ads at viable CPA.

| Condition | Metric | Threshold |
|:---------:|--------|-----------|
| **WIN** | Cost per demo | <$200 over $3,000 spend (min 10 demos) |

## What We Don't Know Yet

- **Demo-to-close rate.** The entire revenue model depends on it, and we have zero data. First 10 demos will set the baseline. If close rate is below 10%, the math changes significantly.
- **Whether parents actually drive school adoption.** The content loop hypothesis (parent finds content -> shares with school -> school buys) is the strategic thesis. It's logical but unproven. Watch for referral source data on demo bookings.
- **Seasonal impact on ad performance.** 504 searches spike Feb-Mar and Aug-Oct. The campaign is launching at a seasonal peak. Summer performance may look very different.
- **Conference ROI.** Speaking is the highest-effort, lowest-volume channel. At $7,500 ACV, even one district deal per conference makes the math work -- but we need to prove it.
- **Pricing sensitivity at the district level.** $7,500/yr for unlimited care teams is the current price. No data on whether districts negotiate, balk, or see it as a bargain. First 5 district conversations will reveal this.
- **SIS integration as a buying requirement.** PowerSchool and Infinite Campus integration is on the roadmap but not built. If "does it integrate with our SIS?" is a dealbreaker rather than a nice-to-have, that changes the build priority.
