# North Star Keyword Themes

> **Status: Draft** | Generated by /context on 2026-03-19

Invincible's keyword opportunity centers on one insight: "504 plan" gets 60,500 searches per month with a competition index of 3-7, and nobody owns it. ## Priority Order (Data-Informed)

1. **504 Plan Hub** — Massive volume (60K+), almost zero competition (index 3-7), and Invincible already has a ranking blog post at position 64. This is the top-of-funnel engine. Content play first, paid later.

2. **Condition-Specific 504** — Strong volume (2.4K for ADHD alone), directly maps to Invincible's founding story and product use case. Parents searching these terms are the champion buyers who go back to their school and say "why don't we have this?"

3. **MTSS/RTI** — Growing category with state mandates. Invincible already ranks #22 for "mtss app." The logbook IS an MTSS documentation tool. Low competition, medium volume.

4. **Competitor Alternatives** — High intent, low volume per term. Worth building comparison pages for Magnus Health, SNAP Health Center, and the compliance tools (Frontline, Embrace, Go504). Different angle: "they write the plan, we execute it."

5. **IEP/SPED Software** — Small but real buyer intent. Invincible complements these tools rather than replacing them. Position as the communication layer that makes IEP execution visible.

6. **School Health Software** — The generic category has near-zero search volume. Not worth leading with. Use as supporting content, not primary strategy.

## Patterns and Observations

Three patterns stand out from the data:

**The competition vacuum is real.** Nearly every high-volume keyword in Themes 1-2 has a competition index under 10. For context, anything under 20 is essentially uncontested. "504 plan" at 60,500/mo with index 7 is the kind of keyword that doesn't stay empty for long — first mover advantage matters here.

**Parent search intent is the hidden growth loop.** Parents search condition-specific 504 terms (ADHD, anxiety, diabetes). No competitor targets parents as buyer influencers. The path is: parent finds Invincible's guide, realizes their school's care coordination is broken, goes back to the school and says "why don't we have this?" This is bottom-up distribution that costs nothing per lead.

**Seasonality creates urgency.** "504 plan" monthly volume ranges from 40,500 (summer) to 110,000 (February, peak IEP season). Back-to-school (Aug-Oct) is the second spike. Content needs to be published well before these windows to rank in time.

## Notes

- **Seasonality is real.** 504 plan searches spike Aug-Oct (back to school) and Feb (IEP season). Monthly volume for "504 plan" ranges from 40,500 (summer) to 110,000 (February).
- **The parent channel is underexploited.** No competitor targets parents as buyer influencers. Parents search condition-specific 504 terms — Invincible can own that path.
- **Content > ads for Themes 1-2.** The informational intent on 504 queries makes SEO the right channel. Paid search makes more sense for Themes 3-5 where intent is commercial.
- **Invincible's existing 504 blog post is the seed.** It ranks position 64 for "what a 504 plan" (4,400/mo). With expansion and optimization, it could crack page 1.
