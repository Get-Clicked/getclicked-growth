## Stage: Early GTM-Fit

Invincible has a live product, paying schools (free tier + small teams), a $3K/mo Google Ads campaign running, and one 504 blog post showing organic signal. The product works. The question is: which distribution channels will produce repeatable, predictable school sign-ups?

**Monthly Budget:** $3,000 (ads) + founder time
**Team:** Founder-led. No dedicated marketing hire.

---

## Inner Ring — Test Immediately

### 1. Content Marketing / SEO (504 Content Engine)

**Why:** "504 plan" gets 60,500 searches/month with a competition index of 3-7 out of 100. Nobody owns it. Invincible already has a blog post ranking at position 64 for "what a 504 plan" (4,400/mo). The parent champion persona searches these terms — they're the catalyst who goes back to their school and asks "why don't we have this?"

The content-to-product pathway is uniquely strong here: parent reads 504 guide, realizes their school's care team communication is broken, discovers Invincible, shares with their school administrator. This is a content loop, not just a content play.

- **Estimated CAC:** $50-150 (content production cost amortized over organic traffic lifetime). Zero marginal cost per visitor once content ranks.
- **Volume:** 60,500/mo for "504 plan" alone. Condition-specific terms add 5,000+/mo. Total addressable organic traffic: 80,000+/mo.
- **Time to signal:** 3-6 months for page 1 rankings. But the existing blog post can be optimized in weeks for a faster first win.
- **Scalability:** Extremely high. Content compounds. 10 articles -> 50 articles -> organic moat.
- **Risk:** Takes time. No instant results. Requires consistent content production.

### 2. SEM / Google Ads (Already Running — Optimize)

**Why:** Already in market at $3K/mo across 3 ad groups. The channel is proven for high-intent buyers — "IEP software" and "SPED software" searchers are actively shopping. Competitor intercept (Frontline, Embrace, Magnus) captures buyers at the evaluation stage. This isn't a new channel test — it's an optimization play.

- **Estimated CAC:** $200-400 based on $8 avg CPC and 2-5% conversion rate. Budget strategy targets CPA < $400.
- **Volume:** Limited by search volume. SPED/IEP software keywords: ~1,500/mo total. Competitor brands: ~35,000/mo but mostly navigational (login intent).
- **Time to signal:** Already running. `/optimize` can pull live data now.
- **Scalability:** Ceiling is low. Total relevant search volume caps around $5K/mo spend before diminishing returns.
- **Risk:** Competitor brand keywords have high navigational intent (people searching "Frontline IEP" want to log in, not switch). Heavy negative keyword management required.

### 3. Speaking Engagements / Conference Strategy

**Why:** EdTech K-12 buying is relationship-driven. District SPED directors and private school admins discover tools through peer recommendations at NASET, CEC, NAIS, and state-level SPED director conferences.

Bob's founding story (diagnosed with type 1 diabetes at 18, interviewed 100+ families) is a natural conference talk. The talk isn't "buy Invincible" — it's "here's what 100 families told me about the gap between the plan and the daily reality." The product sells itself in the Q&A.

- **Estimated CAC:** UNVALIDATED. Depends on conference attendance, booth cost, conversion rate. Likely $500-2,000 per qualified lead, but these are $7,500 district deals.
- **Volume:** 5-20 qualified conversations per conference. 4-6 relevant conferences per year.
- **Time to signal:** Next conference cycle. Can apply to speak at fall conferences now.
- **Scalability:** Low-medium. Founder-dependent. But for $7,500 ACV deals, the math works even at low volume.
- **Risk:** Long sales cycle (spring decisions for fall implementation). Requires founder time.
