## What moved

The first week was all about letting Google's algorithm learn. Week 2 tells a different story.

**SPED/IEP Software (AG1)** is the strongest performer. CTR hit 4.1% -- well above the 3% target. The "504 Plan Execution Software" headline is winning against "SPED Software That Executes" by a 2:1 margin on click-through. Five of the 12 total demos came from this group at $240 CPA. These are district-level buyers searching with commercial intent. The keyword "special education case management" is punching above its weight -- $2.18 CPC against the group average of $8.00.

**District Competitor Intercept (AG2)** is the problem child. CTR is 2.4% -- technically above the 1.5% kill threshold, but 31% of clicks are login-intent queries that slipped through initial negatives. "Frontline iep login," "embrace education sign in," "spedtrack portal" -- people trying to get to their existing tool, not shopping for a new one. Three demos came from this group, but the true CPA after removing navigational waste is closer to $190. The signal is there; the noise is drowning it.

**Private School Health (AG3)** is the sleeper. Only $712 in spend, but 4 demos at $178 CPA. CTR is 3.8%. The "Magnus Health" keyword at $0.66 CPC is delivering clicks at a fraction of the other groups. The "No App Download Needed" headline resonates -- 62% of clicks come from ads featuring it. Small volume, but the economics are the best in the campaign.

## The honest take

The campaign is working. Twelve demos in two weeks on $2,847 is a viable starting point. But the headline number masks a structural problem: a third of your competitor intercept budget is buying login clicks from people who already use Frontline and Embrace. That's not a targeting issue -- it's a negative keyword hygiene issue, and it's fixable today.

The real story is Private School Health. At $68 CPA and 3.8% CTR, that ad group is outperforming the campaign plan by 3x. The "Magnus Health" keyword at $0.66 CPC is essentially free traffic compared to the $8 CPCs in AG1. If you're going to put an extra dollar anywhere, put it here.

Don't scale total budget yet. Clean up the waste first, let the algorithm relearn for a week, then evaluate at the $3K mark (end of Month 1). If CPA holds below $200 after the negative keyword pass, that's your signal to move to $5K/mo per the EXP-001 pre-committed decision.

One more thing: zero demos booked on weekends. Educators work Monday through Friday. Turning off weekend spend is free money.
