## Voice and Tone

### Voice Attributes
| Attribute | What It Means | Example |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| Confident | We state outcomes directly. No hedging, no "might help you." | "Get 2 hours back every day" not "Lindy may help improve your productivity" |
| Outcome-focused | Lead with what changes for the user, not what the product does | "Your SDRs sell more, admin less" not "Our platform automates sales workflows" |
| Anti-hype | Concrete numbers and specific results. Zero buzzwords. | "Set up in 5 minutes, no code required" not "A revolutionary automation solution" |

### Tone Calibration
| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Website copy | Direct, specific, benefit-led. Show the agent in action — screenshots of real workflows, not abstract graphics. |
| Ad headlines | Ultra-specific outcomes. Time saved, tasks handled, setup speed. Every word earns its space (<=30 chars). |
| Blog posts | Expert and practical. "Here's how to automate X" with actual steps, not thought leadership fluff. |
| Social media | Show, don't tell. User stories, before/after time comparisons, specific workflow examples. |
| Email | Personal, concise, one clear ask. Subject lines that state what's inside. |
| Landing pages | One job per page. Headline = outcome. Subhead = how. CTA = specific action. |

### Forbidden Language
- Never use hype terms, inflated language, or business jargon. See the banned word list in PRODUCT_BRIEF.md.
- Never claim: Unquantified "best" or "leading". No "the most powerful AI" without a specific benchmark.
- Never sound: Corporate (no "we strive to provide"), desperate ("limited time offer!"), or vaguely inspirational ("imagine a world where...")
- Never: Position as replacing people. Always frame as "handles the work you shouldn't be doing" — never "replaces your team."

## Brand Guardrails

- Always lead with the outcome, never the technology. "Get 2 hours back" before "AI agent platform."
- Never compare directly to competitors by name in ads — position against the category ("automation tools") instead. Competitor names in organic comparison content are fine.
- Claims must be provable. "40,000+ professionals" is fine (verified). "Best AI agent" is not (unverifiable).
- Always include a specific, tangible proof point within 2 sentences of any claim. "Set up in 5 minutes" -> link to a demo or case study.
- Personas are constraints. When writing for the Overwhelmed Ops Manager, use her language ("repetitive tasks", "no time", "force multiplier"). When writing for the Scaling Sales Leader, use his ("pipeline velocity", "selling time", "close rate").
- The tone test: if Steph (our primary persona) would roll her eyes reading it, rewrite it. She's a smart, experienced professional — don't talk down, don't over-promise, don't be vague.
