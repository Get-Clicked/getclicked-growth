# Overwhelmed Ops Manager

> **Status: Draft** | Generated by /context on 2026-03-19

---

## Demographics

- **Role:** Operations Manager at a 50-200 person startup
- **Age:** 30-42
- **Income:** $85K-$130K
- **Reports to:** COO or CEO
- **Team size:** 3-10 direct reports
- **Tech comfort:** Uses Slack, Google Workspace, Notion daily. Comfortable with tools but not a developer.

---

## Situation

Mid-career ops professional at a company that's scaled past "everyone does everything" but hasn't built out a proper ops team yet. She IS the ops team — plus half the project management, vendor coordination, and internal tooling. The company grew 2x in the last year but headcount didn't keep pace. CEO just asked "what's our AI strategy?" in the last all-hands and she needs an answer that isn't "I'll look into it."

---

## Pain Points

- Spends 3+ hours/day on tasks that don't require human judgment (data entry, calendar wrangling, report generation, vendor follow-ups)
- Tried Zapier — hit the limits fast when she needed workflows with actual reasoning, not just "if this then that"
- Has a backlog of process improvement projects she'll never get to because the daily grind eats all her time
- Hiring freeze means she can't add headcount; needs force multiplier, not another person to manage

---

## How They Search

- Task-specific: "automate meeting scheduling", "AI data entry", "automate report generation"
- Tool comparison: "zapier vs make vs lindy", "best automation tool for startups"
- Problem-first: "too much admin work", "how to automate repetitive tasks"
- Platforms: Google first, then asks in Slack communities (Ops professionals, SaaS Ops)

---

## Objections

- "Will it break my existing workflows?" — integration reliability is table stakes
- "How long to set up?" — if it takes a week of configuration, it's not saving time
- "What about data security?" — ops handles sensitive data (payroll, vendor contracts, financials)
- "My CEO will ask about ROI" — needs a concrete hours-saved or dollar-saved number

---

## What Resonates

- Specific time savings: "Get 2 hours back every day" — she can do the math immediately
- "No code required" — she's not an engineer and doesn't want to become one
- "Set up in 5 minutes" — proves it won't become another project on the backlog
- Case studies from similar-sized companies — social proof that it works at her scale
- Security compliance badges — SOC 2, GDPR = can show CEO it's enterprise-ready

---

## What Falls Flat

- "Transform your operations" — too vague, sounds like a consulting pitch
- "Leverage AI to unlock productivity" — buzzword soup
- "Powerful automation platform" — what does that mean in practice?
- Feature lists without outcomes — she doesn't care about "3,000 integrations", she cares about "connects to your Google Workspace in 30 seconds"

---

## Value Theme Map (Dunford Framework)

| Value Theme | Features | Benefits | Proof |
|-------------|----------|----------|-------|
| **Time recovery** | Pre-built AI agents for email, scheduling, report generation | Gets 2+ hours back per day on tasks that don't require judgment | User-reported: 4 hours/day admin reduced to 8 minutes |
| **Zero learning curve** | Natural language setup, no-code configuration | Deploys in minutes, not weeks — doesn't become another backlog item | "Set up in 5 minutes" — 7-day free trial proves it |
| **Enterprise readiness** | SOC 2, GDPR, HIPAA, PIPEDA compliance, audit logs | She can hand the CEO a concrete security answer when asked about ROI | Compliance certifications + built-in approval workflows |
| **Force multiplication** | AI agents that learn preferences, handle multi-step workflows | Solves the headcount freeze — capacity without hiring | $49.99/mo vs. $25-50/hr for human assistant |
