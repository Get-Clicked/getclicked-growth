## The Three-Channel Strategy

### Channel 1: 504 Content Engine (SEO) — The Growth Loop

**The play:** Become the definitive 504 resource online. 60,500 monthly searches for "504 plan" with competition index 3-7. Nobody owns it.

**The loop:** Parent searches "504 plan for ADHD" -> finds Invincible's guide -> learns about the gap between plan and execution -> shares with school -> school evaluates Invincible -> school signs up -> success story feeds more content -> more parents find it.

This is a **content loop** — the most durable growth engine for Invincible. It compounds. It costs nothing per visitor after production. And it's defensible because it takes a year to build, which means competitors who start later are always behind.

**Budget:** $0 direct (founder time + AI content). Or $500-1,000/mo if outsourcing editing/design.

### Channel 2: Google Ads (SEM) — The Immediate Pipeline

**The play:** Optimize the existing $3K/mo campaign. Cut waste on competitor brand navigational queries. Tighten ad-to-landing-page alignment. The goal isn't scale — it's efficient conversion of high-intent buyers while the content engine builds.

**Budget:** $3,000/mo (existing). Scale to $5K/mo only if CPA < $300.

### Channel 3: Conference Speaking — The Relationship Pipeline

**The play:** Bob's founding story is a conference talk, not a sales pitch. District SPED directors and private school admins discover tools through peer recommendations. Being on stage = instant credibility with the exact buyer persona.

**Budget:** $2,000-5,000 per event (travel + materials). 2-4 events per year.

**Target events:** CEC, NASET, NAIS, and 2-3 state-level SPED conferences.

## The Big Bet

Invincible's distribution advantage is the **parent-to-school influence channel.** No competitor targets parents as buyer influencers. Parents Google "504 plan for ADHD" 1,900 times a month. If Invincible's content helps them, they become champions who ask their school "why aren't we using this?"

The 504 content engine isn't just SEO — it's building an army of parent advocates who do the selling for you. That's a growth loop no competitor can copy by outspending you on ads.

Everything else (ads, conferences, partnerships) is an accelerant. The content engine is the machine.
